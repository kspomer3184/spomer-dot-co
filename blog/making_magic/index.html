Making Magic

Working at Walt Disney World


I started working for The Walt Disney Company in February of 2017 after the research project that I was on exhausted its funding. Being a little tight on money, I accepted a role in Quick Service Food & Beverage, that being in the highest demand at the time. Disney likes to leave an air of mystery around where you are going to be working. All I knew when I accepted the offer was that I was to be working somewhere in the Magic Kingdom. I had no idea that I would end up working in the kitchen of the highest volume (and therefore highest stress) restaurant in the western hemisphere.


The first day of work for all Cast Members working for Walt Disney Parks and Resorts is an all day training called "Disney Traditions". Traditions teaches you about the vision Walt had for the company and how that has been followed through with today. You learn all about the company culture and the subtle things that sets Disney apart from the competition. Following my Traditions day, I went through 5 more days of training about park operations, food and beverage operations and finally my on-the-job training.

After a whirlwind 3 days of location training, I was thrown into work at Cosmic Ray's Starlight Caf√©, one of the busiest kitchens in the world. It took a couple of weeks to get the hang of things, and I was finally able to perform really well. The kitchen at Cosmic Ray's is a very high pressure place. A simple mistake or lapse in judgement can result in thousands of upset guests. There's many positions where you are the only thing preventing a complete standstill in the whole restaurant. It was hard work, but I would much prefer that to idly standing around in a less busy location.

After a month or two I was scheduled my first Greeter shift. The greeter position is untrained, and basically everyone at the restaurant has to do it occasionally. This was my first taste of guest interaction and Making Magic. I got to give stickers to kids, sing birthday songs and give out free food to people who looked like they might have been having a bad day. It was while greeting that I realized just how much I loved working for this company. It helped to give me a direction with what I wanted to do with my career.


In an effort to get more guest interaction than the occasional greeter shift, I signed up for cashier training. The cashier position combined the fast paced work environment of the kitchen with the heavy guest interaction of greeting, and was by far my favorite.

As I got about halfway through the Fall semester of my senior year, I realized that I needed to make a big choice. The amount of time that I was spending at work was starting to impact my performance in classes. Cashiers at Cosmic Ray's were spread thin, getting more frequent and longer shifts than anyone else. I decided that I needed to step away from Disney for a time, so that I could finish off the semester strong. I left the comapany in mid-October.

I interviewed for an Attractions role on the last day of that semester. As I sit writing this, I am five days away from my second (and if all goes well, final) "Disney Traditions" class. I'll be going back to the world's busiest theme park, starting with Frontierland attractions in the coming weeks.
